# Lab 4.3: Stationary Heat Distribution in a Plate

## Work Description
This laboratory work investigates the steady-state heat conduction in a rectangular domain. The physical process is modeled by the **Laplace equation**, which describes how temperature distributes itself until an equilibrium is reached, provided the boundaries are kept at constant (though potentially non-uniform) temperatures.

The objective is to compute this equilibrium state and validate the numerical approach against a theoretical Fourier series solution.

## Mathematical Model
The temperature $u(x, y)$ in a plate $[0, L_x] \times [0, L_y]$ obeys:
$$\frac{\partial^2 u}{\partial x^2} + \frac{\partial^2 u}{\partial y^2} = 0$$
*   **Boundary Conditions:** Dirichlet conditions are applied to the edges. For example, $u(x, L_y) = A \sin(\pi x / L_x)$ and $u(L_x, y) = B \sin(3 \pi y / L_y)$, while other edges are kept at $0$.

### Solution Methods
1.  **Analytical Approach (Fourier Series):**
    The solution is represented as a sum of fundamental modes $(\sin \cdot \sinh)$ that satisfy the boundary conditions. This provides a very high precision reference for validation.
2.  **Numerical Approach (FDM / Relaxation):**
    The domain is discretized into a grid. Each interior point is updated according to the five-point stencil (averaging of neighbors):
    $$u_{i,j} \approx \frac{1}{2(1/\Delta x^2 + 1/\Delta y^2)} \left( \frac{u_{i+1,j} + u_{i-1,j}}{\Delta x^2} + \frac{u_{i,j+1} + u_{i,j-1}}{\Delta y^2} \right)$$
    The algorithm iterates until a predefined tolerance $\epsilon$ is reached.

## Implementation & Results
In `3.6 Plate Heating Laplace.ipynb` (included in Lab 4.3), the explicit relaxation scheme is shown to converge smoothly. The 3D temperature profile visualizes the heat diffusion from the high-temperature boundaries into the cold plate interior. The numerical error relative to the analytical solution remains extremely low ($< 10^{-7}$), demonstrating the robustness of finite difference methods for elliptic PDEs.
