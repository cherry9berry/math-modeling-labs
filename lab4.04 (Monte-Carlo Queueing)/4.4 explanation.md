# Lab 4.4: Monte-Carlo Queueing System Simulation

## Work Description
This laboratory work is dedicated to modeling a Queueing System (SMO) utilizing Monte-Carlo simulations. By relying on random number generation and probabilistic distributions, we simulate the arrival and processing of tasks (or calls) in a multi-channel queue.

The objective is to compare empirical results obtained from the simulation with theoretical values derived from queueing theory, such as Erlang's formulas, and to evaluate system efficiency under varying workloads.

## Mathematical Basis
The simulated system follows an **M/M/c** queue model:
*   **Arrival Process:** Exponentially distributed inter-arrival times.
*   **Service Process:** Exponentially distributed service times.
*   **Channels:** Multiple parallel service channels ($c$).

By playing out thousands of scenarios, the Law of Large Numbers ensures that the average wait times and queue lengths converge to the true expected values.

## Implementation Details
The Jupyter notebook `4.4 Monte-Carlo Queueing.ipynb` contains the full simulation algorithm. 
Key analyses include:
*   Waiting time probabilities.
*   Average queue length.
*   System utilization and bottleneck analysis.
*   SLA (Service Level Agreement) optimization by adjusting the number of available operators.
