# Lab 4.5: Aerodynamic Modeling of NACA Profiles in ANSYS

## Work Description
This laboratory work focuses on the numerical simulation of 2D airflow around various **NACA (National Advisory Committee for Aeronautics)** airfoil profiles using the **ANSYS Fluent** software package. Aerodynamic modeling is crucial for designing wings, turbine blades, and other surfaces where lift-to-drag optimization is required.

The objective is to analyze how different geometric parameters (thickness, camber) affect the aerodynamic coefficients under various flow conditions.

## Mathematical Model
The flow is modeled using the **Reynolds-Averaged Navier-Stokes (RANS)** equations. These equations describe the conservation of mass and momentum for a viscous fluid. To account for turbulence near the airfoil surface, models such as **k-epsilon** or **k-omega SST** are typically employed.

### Simulation Workflow
1.  **Geometry Generation:** Creation of 2D NACA 4-digit or 5-digit coordinates (e.g., NACA 2412).
2.  **Meshing:** Construction of a computational grid, often with inflation layers at the airfoil boundary to accurately capture the boundary layer and flow separation.
3.  **Setup:** Defining fluid properties (air), inlet velocity, and angle of attack ($\alpha$).
4.  **Solution:** Iterative calculation of the pressure and velocity fields until convergence.

## Results Analysis
The primary outputs of the simulation are:
*   **Lift Coefficient ($C_l$):** The force component perpendicular to the flow.
*   **Drag Coefficient ($C_d$):** The resistance force parallel to the flow.
*   **Pressure Distribution ($C_p$):** Visualized as contours around the profile.

The collected data for multiple profiles is stored in `lab_extended_2d_naca_profiles_comparing__.xlsx`, allowing for a comparative analysis of aerodynamic efficiency ($C_l / C_d$).
