# Lab 4.1: Nonlinear Dynamics of a 2D Spring-Mass System

## Work Description
This laboratory work investigates the motion of a mass point on a 2D horizontal table, attached to four symmetrically placed springs. The system exhibits complex nonlinear behavior due to the geometric configuration of the springs, even if the springs themselves are assumed to be linear (Hookean).

The goal is to derive the equations of motion using Lagrangian mechanics and simulate the trajectory of the mass using high-order numerical integration.

## Mathematical Model
Consider a mass $m$ at position $(x, y)$. Each spring $i$ is attached to an anchor $(x_{i0}, y_{i0})$ and has a natural (unstretched) length $L$. The force from one spring is:

```math
\mathbf{F}_i = -k \left( \sqrt{(x-x_{i0})^2 + (y-y_{i0})^2} - L \right) \frac{\mathbf{r} - \mathbf{r}_{i0}}{|\mathbf{r} - \mathbf{r}_{i0}|}
```

### Lagrangian Formulation
The state is defined by coordinates $(x, y)$ and velocities $(\dot{x}, \dot{y})$:
*   **Kinetic Energy:** $T = \frac{1}{2}m(\dot{x}^2 + \dot{y}^2)$
*   **Potential Energy:** $V = \sum \frac{1}{2}k(r_i - L)^2$, where $r_i$ is the distance to the $i$-th anchor.
*   **Equations of Motion:** Derived from the Euler-Lagrange equations $\frac{d}{dt} \frac{\partial L}{\partial \dot{q}} - \frac{\partial L}{\partial q} = 0$. This leads to a system of two second-order ODEs.

### Numerical Solution
The system is solved using the **Runge-Kutta 4th Order (RK4)** method. This method offers a good balance between computational cost and accuracy, preserving the energy of the system better than simple Euler methods over long simulation times.

## Implementation & Results
The notebook `4.1 Springs on Table.ipynb` simulates the system's trajectory from an initial offset. The resulting motion consists of quasi-periodic oscillations. The animation and phase portraits $(x, \dot{x})$ and $(y, \dot{y})$ illustrate how the energy is transferred between the $x$ and $y$ degrees of freedom due to the nonlinear coupling of the springs.
